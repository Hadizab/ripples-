Where water meets sky, and silence speaks

"Somewhere a memory stirred;
Soft as breath, sharp as longing.
And in that fragile stillness,
Truth uncloaked itself,
Asking nothing but to be seen."

From "Midnight Reflections"

HB Hadiza Bagudu
About the Author

Hadiza Bagudu

A visionary poet and storyteller, Hadiza Bagudu crafts verses that explore the spaces between silence and sound, memory and forgetting, love and its echo. Her work invites readers into contemplative landscapes where water reflects stars and midnight holds its breath.

With "Ripples," she offers a collection that moves like water—sometimes still, sometimes trembling, always alive with the reflections of human experience. Her poetry has been described as "luminous," "haunting," and "deeply resonant."
